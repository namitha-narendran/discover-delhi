🍛 Culinary Paradise

Eat your way through Delhi, from legendary lanes to comfort classics

Delhi rewards curiosity. The city can feed you with old-school Mughlai depth, iconic North Indian comfort food, and fast, sharp street flavors within the same neighborhood.

🏢

Chandni Chowk - The Food Capital

Established in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, Chandni Chowk is Asia's largest wholesale market and Delhi's ultimate street food destination. Narrow lanes filled with centuries-old shops serve legendary dishes that have been perfected over generations.

Rule 01

Go where the turnover is high

Busy stalls are a good sign. Delhi locals vote with repetition, and the best places usually have a steady queue.

Rule 02

Mix heavy dishes with lighter snacks

A Delhi food day works best when you alternate full dishes like chole bhature or butter chicken with smaller bites like chaat and kulfi.

Rule 03

Keep your spice curve realistic

Start mildly, drink sealed water, and build upward. Delhi is more fun when your second meal is still possible.
