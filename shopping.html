🛒 Shopper's Paradise

Markets with personality, not just products

Delhi's shopping scene ranges from dense historic bazaars to polished market streets. Some places reward hard bargaining, others reward patience and curation.

Best For

Fashion, crafts, souvenirs

Watch For

Crowds, fake bargains, fatigue

Simple Rule

Use Chandni Chowk and Sarojini for energy and deals. Use Khan Market for polish and a slower pace.

🏢

Chandni Chowk

Historic market maze

Asia's oldest and busiest market. Best for fabric, jewelry, spices, electronics, and wedding shopping.

Tip: bargain hard. Prices can drop fast.

👗

Sarojini Nagar

Budget fashion hunting

Export surplus, accessories, shoes, and trend-driven fashion at aggressive prices.

Tip: weekday mornings are calmer and better stocked.

🎨

Janpath

Easy souvenir stop

Handicrafts, Tibetan jewelry, bohemian clothes, and compact gifting options near Connaught Place.

Tip: ideal if you want memorable pieces without a whole-day market mission.

🏢

Dilli Haat

Crafts from across India

An open-air craft bazaar with regional food stalls, rotating artisans, and a cleaner shopping rhythm.

Tip: excellent for textiles, pottery, and gifts that feel less generic.

📖

Khan Market

Upscale and curated

Boutiques, books, designer wear, and cafes. Better for browsing and quality than bargain-hunting.

Tip: pair it with lunch or coffee, not a rushed visit.

💄

Lajpat Nagar

Ethnic wear and local buys

A favorite for Indian fashion, mehndi, home decor, and affordable market shopping.

Tip: especially strong for festive wear and accessories.
