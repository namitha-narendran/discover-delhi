🏛️ Must Visit Attractions

Delhi's most iconic places, arranged for an actual trip

This page gives the landmark layer of Delhi: Mughal history, ceremonial avenues, modern temples, and the monuments that define the city in a first visit.

Best Pairing

Central Delhi in the morning, heritage sites in the late afternoon, and India Gate after sunset.

Famous Places

Discover Delhi's iconic landmarks that narrate stories of emperors, freedom fighters, and architectural marvels spanning centuries.

Red Fort (Lal Qila)

UNESCO Site

Built in 1639 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, this massive red sandstone fort served as the main residence of the emperors for nearly 200 years. Every Independence Day, India's Prime Minister hoists the national flag here.

📍 Old Delhi     ⏰ 9:30 AM - 4:30 PM

Qutub Minar

UNESCO Site

Standing at 73 meters, this magnificent minaret is the world's tallest brick tower. Built in 1193 by Qutub-ud-din Aibak, its five distinct storeys feature intricate carvings and verses from the Quran.

📍 Mehrauli     ⏰ Sunrise - Sunset

Lotus Temple

Modern Marvel

This stunning Bahai House of Worship resembles a lotus flower with 27 free-standing marble petals. Completed in 1986, it welcomes people of all faiths for meditation and prayer in serene silence.

📍 Nehru Place     ⏰ 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

Humayun's Tomb

UNESCO Site

The inspiration for the Taj Mahal, this stunning garden tomb was built in 1570 for Mughal Emperor Humayun. It introduced the Persian char-bagh (four-quartered garden) style to India's architectural landscape.

📍 Nizamuddin East     ⏰ Sunrise - Sunset

India Gate

War Memorial

This 42-meter high arch commemorates 70,000 Indian soldiers who died in World War I. The Amar Jawan Jyoti burns beneath it, honoring India's unknown soldiers. Perfect for evening visits.

📍 Rajpath     ⏰ 24 Hours

Akshardham Temple

Spiritual

This architectural masterpiece features 20,000 hand-carved statues and intricate stone carvings. The complex includes beautiful gardens, a musical fountain show, and exhibitions on Indian culture and spirituality.

📍 Noida Border     ⏰ 9:30 AM - 6:30 PM

Best Timing

Start early, save your energy

Outdoor monuments are strongest before the heat builds. Keep indoor museums, lunch, and Connaught Place for midday.

Good Pairings

Group by geography

India Gate with Central Delhi. Red Fort with Chandni Chowk. Qutub Minar with Mehrauli or Hauz Khas. That keeps commute fatigue down.

Planning Note

Use the metro whenever possible

Delhi traffic can break an otherwise efficient day. Metro plus short rides for the last mile works best for most travelers.
