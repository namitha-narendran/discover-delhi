📅 Plan Your Trip

A two-day Delhi plan that feels full, not frantic

This route balances New Delhi's formal landmarks with Old Delhi's texture and appetite. It works best for first-time visitors who want a broad read on the city without losing whole hours in transit.

Day 1

Central Delhi and modern avenues

Day 2

Old Delhi and heritage corridors

Planning Principle

Group places by zone. Delhi is generous with experiences but unforgiving about inefficient travel.

D1

Day One

New Delhi and Central Delhi

8:00 AM - 10:00 AM

India Gate and Rajpath

Start with a morning walk at India Gate. Watch the city wake up and enjoy the open lawns before traffic builds.

10:30 AM - 12:00 PM

Rashtrapati Bhavan

Visit the presidential zone and absorb the formal geometry that defines the capital's civic core.

12:30 PM - 2:00 PM

Lunch at Connaught Place

Explore the colonial circles, then reset with lunch at Wenger's or Saravana Bhavan.

3:00 PM - 5:30 PM

National Museum

Use the hotter part of the day indoors and move through Indian history in one well-curated stop.

6:00 PM - 9:00 PM

Evening at India Gate

Return after dark for a completely different mood: lights, street snacks, and families out for the evening.

D2

Day Two

Old Delhi and Heritage Sites

9:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Red Fort

Begin with the Mughal power center and give yourself enough time for the fort's layered interiors and museum spaces.

12:00 PM - 2:30 PM

Chandni Chowk Food Walk

This is the most atmospheric meal block of the trip. Try paranthas, jalebis, lassi, and whatever catches your attention.

3:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Jama Masjid

Visit India's largest mosque and, if open, climb the minaret for a wide view over Old Delhi.

4:30 PM - 6:00 PM

Lotus Temple

End on a calmer note with a serene architectural contrast to the density of Old Delhi.

7:00 PM - 9:00 PM

Shopping and Dinner

Finish at Dilli Haat or a neighborhood restaurant for a relaxed close with handicrafts and regional food.

Pro Tips for Your Trip

🚇

Use Delhi Metro. It connects all major attractions and is still the fastest way to travel across the city.

👟

Wear comfortable walking shoes. Old Delhi especially is best experienced on foot, lane by lane.

📱

Download maps offline. They are especially useful when you are weaving through dense market areas.
